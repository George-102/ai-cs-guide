# A Reference Learning Plan

This is not a rigid curriculum to follow strictly, but a learning roadmap tailored to the needs of CS students in the AI era. Everyone's background, goals, and schedule differ — adapt it to your own situation.

## Design Principles

**Foundation first, AI acceleration second.** Traditional CS fundamentals (data structures, algorithms, operating systems, computer networks) remain hard currency in the AI era. AI tools can accelerate your learning but cannot replace your understanding of the basics.

**Balance theory and practice.** Every course recommends video lectures (theory) + labs/projects (practice). Watching videos without doing projects means you'll forget everything.

**AI tools throughout.** From freshman year, integrate AI-assisted programming, prompt engineering, and similar tools into your daily learning — don't treat them as isolated courses.

## About the CS Entrance Exam (408)

If you plan to take the Chinese CS graduate entrance exam, these four subjects each account for roughly 25 points:

| Subject | Points | Recommended Course |
|---------|--------|-------------------|
| Data Structures | ~25 | [UC Berkeley CS61B](https://sp21.datastructur.es/) |
| Computer Architecture | ~25 | [UC Berkeley CS61C](https://cs61c.org/) |
| Operating Systems | ~25 | [MIT 6.S081](https://pdos.csail.mit.edu/6.828/2023/) |
| Computer Networks | ~25 | [Stanford CS144](https://cs144.github.io/) |

After completing the above courses, use domestic exam prep materials (e.g., Wangdao series) for targeted review. International courses help you understand principles; exam prep materials help you pass the exam — the combination works best.

## Principles for Using AI Tools

1. **AI is an amplifier, not a replacement.** AI helps you write code faster, but responsibility for understanding the code remains yours.
2. **Understand first, then use.** Don't use AI to generate code you don't understand. When you encounter something you don't grasp, ask AI to explain the principle.
3. **Use AI to learn, not to do.** Have AI explain concepts, translate documents, and summarize papers — but thinking and decision-making must be your own.
4. **Keep your coding hands sharp.** Even with AI tools, regularly write code by hand to maintain sensitivity to syntax and algorithms.
