# USTC Zheng Quan: Computer Networks

## Descriptions

- Source: University of Science and Technology of China (Zheng Quan, Yang Jian)
- Prerequisites: Basic computer science
- Difficulty: 🌟�🌟🌟
- Class Hour: 40+

USTC Professor Zheng Quan's *Computer Networks* is one of the best networking courses in China. It's systematic and thorough, particularly suitable for **graduate entrance exam preparation** and **systematic study of networking principles**.

The course covers the complete networking knowledge system, from the physical layer to the application layer, with emphasis on core protocols at the network and transport layers.

## Course Features

- **Systematic**: Layer-by-layer coverage from physical to application
- **Focused**: Network and transport layers are covered in depth
- **Exam-oriented**: Content closely matches the 408 entrance exam syllabus
- **Clear Chinese explanations**: Fluent language with clear concept explanations

## Self-Study Tips

**Pair with Kurose & Ross.** Zheng Quan covers principles; Kurose & Ross provides English perspectives and more examples.

**Do the practice problems.** The course has accompanying exercises, especially useful for exam prep.

**Combine with Wireshark labs.** Use Wireshark to verify protocol principles learned in class.

## Notes

If you're preparing for the 408 entrance exam, Zheng Quan's networking course is essential viewing. Combined with Kurose & Ross, it covers both theory and exam preparation.
