# DDIA: Designing Data-Intensive Applications

## Descriptions

- Source: Martin Kleppmann (University of Cambridge)
- Prerequisites: Database fundamentals, networking fundamentals, OS fundamentals
- Difficulty: 🌟🌟🌟🌟
- Class Hour: 80+

*Designing Data-Intensive Applications* (DDIA) by Martin Kleppmann is considered the "bible" of backend development by countless engineers.

The book's theme: **how to design and build large-scale, reliable, scalable data systems**. It's not a tutorial for any single technology — it builds comprehensive understanding of distributed data systems.

## Resources

- Official: [dataintensive.net](https://dataintensive.net/)

## Core Content

### Part 1: Foundations of Data Systems
- Data models and query languages
- Storage and retrieval (B+ trees, LSM trees, column stores)
- Encoding and evolution (JSON, Protobuf, Avro, Thrift)

### Part 2: Distributed Data
- Replication (primary-replica, multi-leader, leaderless)
- Partitioning (hash, range)
- Distributed transactions and consensus (2PC, Paxos, Raft)

### Part 3: Derived Data
- Batch processing (MapReduce, Spark)
- Stream processing (Kafka, Flink)
- The future of data systems

## Self-Study Tips

**Build foundations first.** The book assumes knowledge of basic databases and networking.

**Read by topic, not sequentially.** Choose chapters based on your needs.

**Do the review questions.** Each chapter has questions to test your understanding.

## Notes

DDIA is essential reading for backend engineers. Without understanding it, you can only call APIs; with it, you can design systems.

It's also a reference for system design interviews at many major companies.
