# Sutton & Barto: Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction

## Descriptions

- Source: Richard Sutton, Andrew Barto (University of Alberta)
- Prerequisites: ML basics, probability, calculus
- Difficulty: 🌟🌟🌟🌟
- Class Hour: 80+

Sutton & Barto's *RL: An Introduction* is the "bible" of RL. Richard Sutton is known as the "father of reinforcement learning".

## Resources

- Website: [incompleteideas.net](http://incompleteideas.net/book/the-book-2nd.html)
- Free online reading
- Code: [GitHub](https://github.com/ShangtongZhang/reinforcement-learning-an-introduction)

## Content

- Part 1: Tabular Methods (Bandits, MDP, DP, MC, TD)
- Part 2: Approximate Methods (Function Approximation, Policy Gradient)
- Part 3: Frontiers (Exploration, Multi-Agent RL)

## Tips

Read after David Silver. The book is theory-heavy; build intuition first.

## Notes

Essential reading for RL researchers. Required for deep theoretical understanding of RL.
