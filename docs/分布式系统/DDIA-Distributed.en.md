# DDIA: Distributed Systems Chapters

## Descriptions

- Source: Martin Kleppmann *Designing Data-Intensive Applications*
- Prerequisites: Database fundamentals, networking fundamentals
- Difficulty: 🌟🌟🌟🌟
- Class Hour: 40+

DDIA Part 2 "Distributed Data" is the most valuable section of the book and one of the best reading materials for getting started with distributed systems.

## Core Chapters

### Chapters 5-6: Replication
- Primary-replica, multi-leader, leaderless replication
- Handling replication lag

### Chapters 7-8: Partitioning
- Hash partitioning, range partitioning
- Secondary index partitioning
- Partition rebalancing

### Chapter 9: Distributed Transactions and Consensus
- Two-Phase Commit (2PC)
- Three-Phase Commit (3PC)
- Paxos, Raft
- Linearizability

## Self-Study Tips

**Read Chapters 5-6 (Replication) first.** Replication is the most fundamental concept in distributed systems.

**Chapter 9 is the highlight.** Distributed transactions and consensus algorithms are interview favorites.

**Pair with MIT 6.824.** DDIA covers theory; 6.824 provides hands-on labs.

## Notes

DDIA's distributed section is more accessible than most distributed systems textbooks. If you find textbooks too theoretical, start with DDIA to build intuition.
