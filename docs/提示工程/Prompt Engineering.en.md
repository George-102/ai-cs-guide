Prompt engineering, in simple terms, is a practical discipline that **studies how to communicate more effectively with large language models (such as me) to obtain high-quality and expected results**. It is not just about writing prompts, but also a systematic method for designing, optimizing, and managing interactions. For computer science students, the core value of learning it lies in the fact that **it is a crucial bridge connecting powerful AI capabilities with real-world applications, and is becoming a new and essential paradigm of human-computer interaction**. 

The following is the introduction from the official website:

>Prompt engineering is a  relatively new discipline for developing and optimizing prompts to  efficiently use language models (LMs) for a wide variety of applications and research topics. Prompt engineering skills help to better  understand the capabilities and limitations of large language models  (LLMs).
>
>Researchers use prompt  engineering to improve the capacity of LLMs on a wide range of common  and complex tasks such as question answering and arithmetic reasoning.  Developers use prompt engineering to design robust and effective  prompting techniques that interface with LLMs and other tools.
>
>Prompt engineering is not  just about designing and developing prompts. It encompasses a wide range of skills and techniques that are useful for interacting and developing with LLMs. It's an important skill to interface, build with, and  understand capabilities of LLMs. You can use prompt engineering to  improve safety of LLMs and build new capabilities like augmenting LLMs  with domain knowledge and external tools.

The document elaborates on various technical concepts and applications of prompt engineering, with content that is straightforward and easy to understand. It is highly suitable for users who are new to prompt engineering and aspire to have positive interactions with AI.
