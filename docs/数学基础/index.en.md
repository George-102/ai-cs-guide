# Mathematics Foundation Overview

## Why CS Students Need Math

Mathematics is the foundation of computer science. Algorithms, machine learning, computer graphics, cryptography — all require solid mathematical foundations. The AI era demands even more mathematical understanding than before.

## Core Courses

### Calculus
The foundation of understanding change and optimization. Gradient descent, backpropagation, probability density functions — all built on calculus.

### Linear Algebra
The "language" of AI. Matrix operations, eigenvalues, SVD — the core operations of deep learning, computer vision, and NLP.

### Probability and Statistics
The foundation of understanding uncertainty. Bayesian inference, maximum likelihood estimation, hypothesis testing — the theoretical framework of machine learning.

### Discrete Mathematics
The formal language of computer science. Logic, set theory, graph theory, combinatorics — foundations of algorithm analysis, data structures, and cryptography.

## Learning Path

**Freshman Fall**: Calculus I + Linear Algebra
**Freshman Spring**: Calculus II + Discrete Math
**Sophomore Fall**: Probability and Statistics

If time is limited, priority: **Linear Algebra > Calculus > Probability > Discrete Math**.
