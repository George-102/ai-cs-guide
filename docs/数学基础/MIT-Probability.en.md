# MIT 6.041: Probabilistic Systems Analysis and Applied Probability

## Descriptions

- Offered by: MIT
- Prerequisites: Calculus (18.01 level)
- Difficulty: 🌟🌟🌟
- Class Hour: 60+

MIT 6.041 is MIT's probability theory and statistics course. Probability theory is the theoretical framework of machine learning — Bayesian inference, maximum likelihood estimation, hypothesis testing are all core AI tools.

## Course Resources

- Website: [ocw.mit.edu/6.041](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-041sc-probabilistic-systems-analysis-and-applied-probability-fall-2013/)
- Lectures: [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLUl4u3cNGP60hI9ATjSFgLZpbNJ7myAg6)
- Textbook: *Introduction to Probability* (Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis)

## Course Content

### Part 1: Probability Foundations
- Sample spaces and events
- Conditional probability and independence
- Bayes' theorem

### Part 2: Random Variables
- Discrete random variables (Bernoulli, Binomial, Poisson, Geometric)
- Continuous random variables (Uniform, Normal, Exponential)
- Expectation and variance
- Joint and conditional distributions

### Part 3: Limit Theorems
- Law of large numbers
- Central limit theorem

### Part 4: Statistical Inference
- Maximum likelihood estimation
- Bayesian inference
- Hypothesis testing
- Regression analysis

### Part 5: Stochastic Processes (Advanced)
- Markov chains
- Poisson processes

## Self-Study Tips

**Learn calculus before probability.** Probability theory requires integration skills from calculus.

**Focus on Bayes' theorem and maximum likelihood estimation.** These concepts appear everywhere in machine learning.

**Do lots of probability problems.** The core of learning probability is building intuition, not memorizing formulas.

## Notes

MIT 6.041 is one of the best introductory probability courses. Professor John Tsitsiklis is a top researcher in probability theory.

If you're pursuing deep learning research, consider studying stochastic processes (MIT 6.262) — Markov chains are crucial in reinforcement learning.
