# Paul's Online Math Notes

## Descriptions

- Source: Paul Dawkins (Lamar University)
- Prerequisites: High school math
- Difficulty: 🌟🌟 ~ 🌟🌟🌟
- Class Hour: Varies

Paul's Online Math Notes is a free math notes series written by Professor Paul Dawkins of Lamar University. It is one of the best free notes for university-level calculus and linear algebra.

The notes are known for being **detailed, clear, with extensive worked examples**. Unlike textbooks that emphasize axiomatic derivations, Paul's notes focus on "how to compute" and "how to apply."

## Content Structure

### Calculus I
- Limits and continuity
- Derivatives and differentiation
- Applications of derivatives
- Integration
- Applications of integration

### Calculus II
- Techniques of integration
- Parametric equations and polar coordinates
- Infinite series
- Taylor series

### Calculus III
- Vectors and analytic geometry
- Partial derivatives
- Multiple integrals
- Line and surface integrals

### Linear Algebra
- Linear systems and matrices
- Determinants
- Vector spaces
- Eigenvalues and eigenvectors

## Self-Study Tips

**Use alongside formal courses.** Paul's notes are best for review after watching formal lecture videos.

**Focus on the worked examples.** The notes' greatest value is the extensive collection of detailed examples.

**Do the practice problems.** Each chapter has practice problems with solutions.

## Notes

Paul's Online Math Notes is recommended by many students in China for graduate entrance exam preparation. The examples are moderately difficult and great for exam prep.
